# 08. R3 Apparent Infeasibility Revision

## Definition

The R3 column initially looks like a task-infeasible or extreme-rotation failure regime because latent CEM, V3, and especially V2 often stay very low. V1 hinge oracle CEM revises that reading: several R3 cells become highly successful under a success-aligned cost with outside-region gradient. This page records the apparent-infeasibility motif and the Phase 1 evidence that prevents treating it as a confirmed infeasibility mechanism.

## Regimes Affected

- R3 cells: D0xR3, D1xR3, D2xR3, D3xR3.
- Associated all-fail Track A pairs include R3 representatives from D0, D1, D2, and D3.
- Representative pair IDs: `18`, `42`, `67`, `93`, `98`.

## Key Numbers

- D1xR3 latent=`0.0%`, V3=`0.8%`, V2=`0.0%`, but V1=`50.8%`.
- D2xR3 latent=`0.7%`, V3=`0.0%`, V2=`0.0%`, but V1=`71.4%`.
- D3xR3 latent=`7.1%`, V3=`35.7%`, V2=`0.0%`, but V1=`99.3%`.
- D0xR3 latent=`16.7%`, V3=`16.7%`, V2=`33.3%`, V1=`66.7%`.
