\section{Architecture-Matched Nulls}

Section~4 shows that endpoint ranking and CEM-pool ranking can decouple. We next ask whether the endpoint signal itself is an artifact of random high-dimensional geometry rather than learned LeWM structure.

It is not. The trained PushT LeWM encoder reaches $\Rendpoint=0.506$ on the Track~A endpoint artifact. Uninformative nulls stay near zero: Gaussian features reach $-0.006$ and shuffled latents reach $0.004$. The architecture-matched C6 control, a randomly initialized ViT-tiny with the same endpoint protocol, does not reproduce the learned pattern; on PushT it is actively anti-correlated ($-0.200$ for seed~0; $-0.156 \pm 0.086$ across 10 seeds). Details of C6 seeds, projector checks, eval/train mode, raw-pixel baselines, and cross-architecture controls are in Appendix~\ref{app:c6_audit}.

The cross-environment check reaches the same conclusion without the PushT inversion. Cube C6 is weakly positive ($+0.115 \pm 0.016$), while trained Cube LeWM reaches $+0.604$ from a near-zero raw-pixel baseline. Thus random projections and random architecture-matched encoders do not produce the observed endpoint geometry; the learned representation is essential. Sections~4 and~6 then show that this learned endpoint geometry is still insufficient for CEM-local planning.
